Probability-based Adaptive Sampling (PAS) Algorithm Implementation

Tactile Sensing Paper (Under Review)

  • Research focuses on tactile sensing, specifically on increasing the speed of non-parallelized sensing grids by implementing a new algorithm called Probability-based Adaptive Sampling (PAS). The principle behind PAS is to smartly sample fewer pixels per frame and rather sample pixels near regions of high pressure.
  • The algorithm maps each pixel in the grid to a probability, and increases that probability if that pixel is near a region of recurrent high pressure. Unsampled pixels are then reconstructed using a sampling dictionary to produce the final tactile image with enhanced accuracy and reduced computational overhead.
